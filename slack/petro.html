Petro

There are over 10,000 species of jellyfish but ours is a robot. Jellyfish have drifted in ocean currents for millions of years. Petro likes swimming in a sea of storage so you don’t have to.

These transparent creatures are found in channels across the globe. From the surface of Slack to the deep seas of Dropbox and Google Drive. Integrating into your existing workflow as only a faithful invertebrate can do.

Jelly fish don’t have brains. But Petro’s nerve net senses changes in your environment and he coordinates the responses. His gelatinous body contains 98% water allowing Petro to shift shapes and forms to your needs.

Become like water my friend.

Petro is carnivorous, feeding on Keynote and PowerPoint presentations. He hunts passively with trailing tentacles wrapped around your decks. This jellyfish injects venom into collaboration problems of the past while propelling updates right to you.

Some jellyfish are immortal. When stressed they transform back into a polyp and begin life anew. Petro is not quite as persistent. Although we never want to see you go you can revoke all permissions and authorizations with the push of a button.

Jellyfish are the most energy efficient swimmers of all animals. Petro helps you become more effective by swimming through data allowing focus on more important matters. With version control over collaboration predators, you too will rise to the top of the food chain.
